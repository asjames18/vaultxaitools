## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: AI Agents — Build, Deploy, and Scale
Core technical pillar. Everything about designing, building, and deploying AI agents — from single-task agents to multi-agent orchestration systems.

Subtopics: Agent architecture patterns (ReAct, Plan-and-Execute, Reflexion), building with LangChain/LangGraph/CrewAI/AutoGen, agent memory systems, tool use and function calling, observability, multi-agent orchestration, testing frameworks, production deployment.

### Pillar 2: MCP Servers — The Agent Infrastructure Layer
Model Context Protocol is the emerging standard for connecting AI agents to tools and services. First-mover SEO opportunity — low competition, rapidly growing search volume.

Subtopics: What MCP is and why it matters, building MCP servers (Python + TypeScript), security and authentication, connecting to databases and APIs, publishing to registries, custom servers for business workflows, enterprise MCP deployments, monetization.

### Pillar 3: Prompt Engineering — From Amateur to Architect
Goes beyond surface-level tips into systematic prompt architecture for production systems.

Subtopics: System prompt design, chain-of-thought, few-shot strategies, structured output prompting (JSON/XML), prompt chaining, role/persona prompting, prompt security, evaluation and testing, model-specific differences.

### Pillar 4: AI Automation — Workflows That Work While You Sleep
Practical automation for business operators. No-code and low-code tools alongside custom Python scripts.

Subtopics: n8n, Make, Zapier AI integrations, custom automation with Python, CRM and sales automation, content pipelines, email and outreach automation, customer support automation.

### Pillar 5: Local AI — Run AI on Your Own Hardware
Fast-growing search volume, privacy-conscious audience, low competition on specific terms.

Subtopics: Running Ollama on Mac/Windows/Linux, best local models, LM Studio, hardware requirements, local AI apps with Python, privacy and security, fine-tuning locally, local vs. cloud cost comparison.

### Pillar 6: Agent Skills & Tools
The ecosystem of tools, plugins, and extensions that make agents more capable.

Subtopics: Claude Code skills, browser automation with Playwright, web scraping for agents, code execution tools, search tools (Tavily, Exa), file system tools, communication integrations, building a personal skill library.

### Pillar 7: AI Business Solutions — Build Products, Not Just Projects
Bridges technical and entrepreneurial interests — helping readers turn AI skills into income.

Subtopics: AI consulting packaging, AI-powered SaaS, pricing AI services, finding first clients, agency business model, white-labeling tools, case studies of Black AI entrepreneurs, revenue models.

### Pillar 8: Agent Fleets & Enterprise AI — Scaling Intelligence
Advanced pillar targeting senior engineers, technical founders, and enterprise practitioners.

Subtopics: Multi-agent fleet architectures, fleet monitoring and management, cost optimization, enterprise AI governance, agent-to-agent communication protocols, parallel execution patterns, internal AI platforms.

## Author Strategy

**Tier 1 — Core Team Content (40%):** Long-form cornerstones, flagship tutorials, thought leadership. Written by founder or core team. Highest quality and original voice.

**Tier 2 — AI-Assisted Content (40%):** Claude as research and drafting assistant. Human editor refines, adds voice, verifies accuracy, adds original examples. Makes high-volume publishing sustainable.

AI-assisted content disclosure: Melanated In Tech is an AI platform — using AI to help produce content is on-brand and authentic, not something to hide. Standard: AI does the labor, humans provide the intelligence and perspective.

**Tier 3 — Community Contributors (20%):** Guest posts from community members who have built something real. Require original projects and genuine experience. Provides diverse perspectives and author promotional traffic.

## Newsletter Strategy

**Name:** "The Stack" — practical AI intelligence for builders

**Format:** Weekly (Tuesday), 800–1,200 words

**Structure:**
1. Editor's Note (2–3 sentences, founder voice)
2. This Week in AI (3 curated news items with commentary)
3. Tutorial or Deep Dive (excerpt from latest content)
4. Tool of the Week (one MIT-curated tool with use case)
5. Community Spotlight (member project or win)
6. Job/Opportunity Board (AI roles, client opportunities)
7. CTA (always one; rotates between join community, enroll in course, browse marketplace)

**Lead Magnet:** "MIT AI Agent Starter Guide" — 20-page PDF covering what agents are, how to build one, and 10 use cases. Gates the email signup and increases conversion rate.

**Segmentation:** Eventually segment into two lists: "Tools Digest" (weekly tool roundup) and "Builder's Brief" (tutorials + strategy). Let users self-select on signup.

**Growth target:** 500 subscribers by Day 30, 1,000 by Day 60, 2,000 by Day 90.
