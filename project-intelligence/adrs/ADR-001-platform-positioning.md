Every decision should move the platform closer to becoming the trusted place where people learn, build, deploy, buy, sell, and scale AI-powered systems.

# ADR-001: Platform Positioning — Built Specifically for Black/Melanated AI Builders

**Date:** 2026-06
**Status:** Active
**Deciders:** Antonio James (Founder)

---

## Context

The AI education market is enormous ($4.8B in 2024, growing to $32B by 2030) and increasingly crowded with generic platforms (Coursera, Udemy, DeepLearning.AI, Maven). These platforms serve everyone, which means they serve no one exceptionally well.

At the same time, the AI economy is creating unprecedented wealth-building opportunities — but without deliberate intervention, this wealth transfer will happen without meaningful Black ownership participation. Black professionals represent ~13% of the US population but a fraction of AI infrastructure ownership.

The question was: should Melanated In Tech be a general-purpose AI platform that happens to be founded by a Black person, or should it be explicitly positioned as a platform for Black and melanated professionals building AI-powered businesses?

---

## Decision

**Build explicitly for Black and melanated professionals.** The platform's positioning, messaging, community culture, curriculum design, and marketing will center the economic empowerment of Black builders.

The brand promise: *"We give you everything you need to go from AI curious to AI sovereign."*

This is not a restriction — it is a strategic differentiator. The secondary audience statement: "Everyone who believes in economic empowerment through tech ownership is welcome." But the primary audience is unambiguous.

---

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative A: General-Purpose AI Platform
Build without explicit cultural positioning. Target all developers and professionals. Compete directly with Coursera, DeepLearning.AI.

**Why rejected:** General platforms have a 5–10 year head start, enormous capital, and name recognition. MIT would be outspent and out-SEO'd in the generic market. There is no differentiated reason to choose MIT over these incumbents without the cultural specificity.

### Alternative B: Black Tech Community Without AI Focus
Build a general Black tech community/platform (like dev/color or NSBE) and add AI content over time.

**Why rejected:** The AI opportunity is time-sensitive. The first platform to establish authority as the AI builder platform for Black professionals will have a durable moat. Waiting to add AI focus wastes the window.

### Alternative C: Black-Focused But Not Explicitly Stated
Build the platform with cultural sensitivity but don't lead with the identity positioning in marketing.

**Why rejected:** Implicit positioning is not positioning. The cultural trust moat only works if it's explicit and authentic. Incumbents cannot replicate genuine cultural alignment that is built-in from Day 1.

---

## Why Chosen

1. **Cultural trust is a moat incumbents cannot manufacture.** A large incumbent can copy curriculum. They cannot copy cultural trust earned through authentic community building over years.

2. **Network effects compound faster in a focused community.** 1,000 deeply aligned members create more value than 10,000 loosely connected ones. Focused communities have higher retention, stronger referral loops, and more authentic social proof.

3. **The market is underserved, not saturated.** No platform exists that combines: cultural alignment + technical depth + marketplace monetization + community infrastructure specifically for Black professionals building AI.

4. **Identity is the acquisition engine.** The story "platform built by and for Black AI builders" is inherently newsworthy and shareable in ways generic platforms are not. It generates earned media, community referrals, and partnership opportunities that generic positioning never would.

---

## Tradeoffs

| Accepted Tradeoff | Reasoning |
|------------------|----------|
| Smaller initial addressable market | Depth > breadth in early stages. $28–42M ARR SAM is still large. |
| Some potential users may self-select out | Anyone not aligned with the mission is not the target buyer anyway |
| Higher cultural responsibility | The platform must live up to what it claims. Community trust is earned, not bought. |
| Cannot pivot away from identity without community damage | The positioning is a commitment, not a tactic |

---

## Future Impact

**This decision enables:**
- Authentic earned media and PR opportunities
- Deep community trust that creates retention without incentives
- Partnership opportunities with HBCUs, CBOs, Black enterprise networks
- Cultural authority that becomes a barrier to entry for any copycat
- Consulting and enterprise sales rooted in genuine expertise and trust

**This decision constrains:**
- Marketing budget must be focused on channels where the target audience lives (LinkedIn, X, specific Black tech communities, etc.)
- Product decisions must consistently serve the primary audience first
- Brand partnerships must align with values (no sponsorship from companies with poor DEI records)

**Metric to watch:** Community NPS score. If community members would not recommend MIT to other Black professionals, the positioning is not working.
