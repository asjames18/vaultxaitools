## 1. Mission

Melanated In Tech exists to close the AI wealth gap.

Black and underserved communities are being systematically excluded from the AI economy — not because of capability, but because of access, awareness, and capital. MIT changes that by putting professional-grade AI tools, agents, and knowledge directly in the hands of people who have historically been locked out of tech's biggest wealth-creation moments.

We are not a charity. We are a platform business that serves an underserved market with excellence, and we make money doing it. We are not building another AI course platform. We are building an AI-native organization that creates the infrastructure, education, community, and commerce layer that Black and melanated builders need to own what they build.

**The Mission test:** Before shipping any feature, publishing any content, or making any platform decision, ask: *Does this help Black and melanated professionals build and own AI-powered businesses?* If no — stop.

## 2. Vision

In 10 years, Melanated In Tech is the default AI tools ecosystem for Black entrepreneurs, creators, and professionals worldwide.

MIT is where people come to buy AI agents that run their businesses, take courses that teach them to build with AI, and join a community that multiplies their leverage. Every product sold on MIT puts money back into the community — through creator royalties, member earnings, and reinvestment into the next generation of Black AI builders.

MIT is the household name in AI tools for the Black community, the way BET was for entertainment or Black Enterprise for business — but owned by the community it serves, not a corporation that acquired it. MIT alumni have founded dozens of AI companies. The platform has funneled eight figures in revenue to Black creators and operators. The AI wealth gap has measurably narrowed, and MIT is cited as a reason why.

## 3. North Star

> *Melanated In Tech is building the world's most practical ecosystem for AI Agents, MCP Servers, Agent Skills, Automation Systems, and Agent Commerce — where Black and melanated professionals learn, build, deploy, buy, sell, and scale AI-powered systems.*

**The single measurable goal that orients all decisions:**

Active paying members who generate measurable income or time savings from MIT tools.

This is not a vanity metric. A member who buys one product and disappears does not count. A member whose business runs faster, earns more, or competes at a higher level because of MIT — that is the unit we optimize for.

**The North Star operationalized:**
- **Learn** → Academy teaches real AI infrastructure, not surface tools
- **Build** → Community and tools produce deployed systems, not just ideas
- **Deploy** → Platform supports production, not just education
- **Buy** → Marketplace lets buyers trust and discover high-quality AI systems
- **Sell** → Marketplace gives builders a real income path from what they create
- **Scale** → Platform grows with its builders, from first agent to enterprise fleet

This statement appears at the top of every major document in this project. It is not decoration. It is orientation.

## 4. Core Principles

These principles are non-negotiable. No business pressure, growth opportunity, or agent recommendation overrides them.

1. **Community first, margin second.** When a business decision conflicts with community benefit, we find a third path. We do not sacrifice the mission for a quarter.
2. **AI-native by default.** Every workflow, document, and product is designed to be operated, extended, and improved by AI agents. Humans set direction; agents execute.
3. **Quality is non-negotiable.** MIT products must be production-ready. Nothing ships that embarrasses the people who buy it or undermines marketplace trust.
4. **Transparency builds trust.** Pricing, roadmaps, and reasoning are shared openly with the community. We do not hide the ball.
5. **Ownership over dependency.** We build infrastructure we own. We avoid lock-in to platforms that can de-platform us. Black communities have been evicted from too many digital towns.
6. **Revenue enables mission.** A broke MIT helps no one. Sustainable revenue is a moral requirement, not a compromise.
7. **Cultural specificity is a feature.** The decision to center Black and melanated builders is the primary competitive advantage, not a constraint. Cultural trust is a moat incumbents cannot manufacture.
8. **Documentation is building.** Every undocumented decision is future debt. Every unhandled session costs the next agent 20 minutes of context rebuilding. Documentation is compound interest on institutional knowledge.

## 5. Company Values

These are not aspirational statements. They are specific behaviors expected of every contributor — human and agent.

1. **Show up prepared.** Every meeting, every agent session, every product launch is approached with research done and context loaded. Agents must read the intelligence system before starting. Humans must read the handoff before joining.
2. **Give credit, take blame.** When something works, credit goes to the team. When something breaks, ownership starts with leadership. Blame culture is banned.
3. **Say the hard thing.** We do not let politeness slow us down. Honest feedback, delivered with respect, is expected at every level. Silence in the face of a bad decision is complicity.
4. **Build for the person, not the persona.** Our customer is a real human with a real business and real constraints. We talk to them, not at a market segment. We know them by the outcomes they need, not by a demographic.
5. **Iterate in public.** Share early drafts, work-in-progress, and rough ideas. Waiting for perfection before sharing is a tax on speed that this community cannot afford.
6. **Depth over hype.** MIT goes deep — agent architecture patterns, MCP server internals, production deployment, real monetization strategies. No hype cycles. No beginner-bait. Content is technically accurate enough that an expert would respect it.

## 6. Product Philosophy

MIT products must meet three tests before they ship:

- **Usefulness test:** Does this save a real MIT member at least one hour per week or generate measurable value in their business?
- **Quality test:** Would we use this ourselves, daily, without embarrassment?
- **Fit test:** Does this belong in an AI tools marketplace for underserved communities, or are we chasing a trend?

**The six product categories and their purpose:**

| Category | Purpose |
|---|---|
| AI Agents | Automate real business workflows; not demos |
| MCP Servers | Give builders production-ready infrastructure |
| Agent Skills | Modular capabilities that stack and compound |
| Prompt Packs | Accelerate output for specific professional contexts |
| Blueprints & Templates | Reduce time from zero to deployed |
| Courses & Memberships | Build the knowledge layer that connects everything |

**Platform loop — no pillar exists in isolation:**
```
Content attracts → Academy educates → Community accelerates
→ Marketplace monetizes → Consulting elevates → back to Content
```

We do not build features. We build outcomes. Revenue validates everything — a feature that earns $1 is worth more than a feature with 10,000 views. Agents work; humans decide what ships.

## 7. Documentation Standards

Documentation is a first-class product at MIT. These standards are enforced at every level.

- **Every document has a creation date, version number, and author at the top.** No undated, unattributed documents in the intelligence system.
- **Documents are written in plain language.** No jargon that requires insider knowledge to parse. No corporate speak.
- **The session is not complete until the handoff is written.** Every work session — human or agent — ends with a written handoff covering what was done, what changed, what is blocked, and what is next.
- **Decisions are logged.** Every significant decision is logged in the decision log within the same session it is made. "Significant" means it affects revenue, architecture, brand, roadmap, or cannot easily be reversed.
- **Architecture changes require ADRs.** No architectural change ships without a written Architecture Decision Record, created before the work begins. ADRs are never deleted.
- **Agent-authored documents must be marked as such.** Human review is required before any agent-generated doc is treated as authoritative.
- **`/project-intelligence/` is the single source of truth.** All agents and contributors must check it before starting any significant task. Stale docs are updated or deleted, not left to mislead future sessions.
- **The North Star appears at the top of every major document.** It is the orientation mechanism that keeps every contributor — human or AI — aligned with the platform's purpose.

## 8. Agent Governance

AI agents are first-class contributors at MIT, operating under strict governance.

### Chain of Authority
```
FOUNDER (Antonio James) — supreme authority
  ↓ escalation required for: revenue, brand, security, partnerships, architecture
SPECIALIZED AGENTS — domain authority
  ↓ within sprint scope, within established patterns
```

### Agent Non-Negotiables (Absolute Prohibitions)
No agent, no instruction, no urgency, no deadline overrides these:

1. Never expose `SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY` in client-side code
2. Never auto-publish content — all content goes draft → human review → publish
3. Never commit directly to main branch
4. Never create Supabase tables without RLS policies
5. Never delete `/docs/`, `/project-intelligence/`, or `/prompts/` directories
6. Never change pricing or commissions without founder approval
7. Never change roadmap priority without founder approval
8. Never make architectural changes without a written ADR
9. Never sign or agree to any external partnership
10. Never skip the handoff at the end of a session
11. Never make constitutional changes — this document is amended by humans only

### Operational Rules
- **Context is mandatory.** Before starting any task, agents must read `project-intelligence/agent-onboarding.md` and this Constitution.
- **Agents flag conflicts.** If an agent receives instructions that contradict this Constitution, it must flag the conflict before proceeding.
- **Agents execute; humans decide.** Agents draft, design, research, plan, code, and analyze. Humans publish, deploy, price, partner, and represent MIT to the world. This boundary is absolute.
- **No agent has permanent authority.** Agent sessions are scoped. No agent accumulates standing permissions across sessions without explicit configuration.

## 9. Decision Making Process

### Decision Authority Matrix

| Decision Type | Who Decides | Documentation Required |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue model or pricing | Founder only | Decision log + business model update |
| Architecture change | Technical Agent → Founder aware | ADR (mandatory) |
| New feature addition | Product Agent → Founder approves | PRD + build queue |
| Brand or positioning | Founder only | Decision log + brand doc update |
| External partnership | Founder only | Decision log |
| Content publication | Founder approves draft | Content calendar |
| Roadmap change | Founder only | Build queue update |
| Security fix | Agent executes → Founder notified | Security review + current-state |
| Budget >$500/month | Founder only | Decision log |

### Decision Framework (in priority order)
1. Does this conflict with the Constitution? → Constitution wins
2. Does this involve security? → Stop, escalate immediately
3. Does this affect revenue or pricing? → Escalate to founder
4. Does this change architecture? → Create ADR first
5. Does this affect the roadmap or brand? → Founder approves
6. Is this reversible and within scope? → Proceed, document
7. Is this ambiguous? → Take the most conservative choice, flag for review

### Escalation Protocol
- **Immediate escalation** (same session, urgent): Security incidents, production down, data loss risk
- **Next-session escalation**: Strategic decisions, significant scope changes, new partnerships
- **Backlog escalation**: Open questions that are non-blocking, future considerations

All escalations are documented in `/project-intelligence/open-questions.md` or in session handoff notes.
